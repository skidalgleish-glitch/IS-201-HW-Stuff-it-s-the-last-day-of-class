Menu: How to Survive a Separatist Attack

Threat Overview

CIS Profile

Expect massed droid formations supported by armor and ruthless commanders. Typical forces include line-infantry B1 units (numerous but brittle), heavier B2 squads (with sustained fire), and rolling droidekas utilizing personal shields and high-rate blasters. Commanders like General Grievous favor rapid flanking, shock pressure, and exploitation of weak sectors. Corporate backers field mechanized support and standardized tactics, which you can predict—and exploit.

  • Mass Fire: Overwhelm via volume, not precision.
  • Shield Tech: Droideka bubbles resist blaster fire.
  • Rigid Scripts: Patrol routes & arcs are predictable.
  • Fast Flanks: Grievous pressures your weak sides.

Preparation Checklist

  • Map your cover: Identify hard cover, choke points, and fallback routes in advance.
  • Signal plan: Pre-assign hand signals & rally points; carry beacons or glow markers.
  • Ion/EMP options: Pack an ion flare or EMP charge for shielded targets.
  • Comms discipline: Short bursts, clear brevity; avoid open chatter that droids can triangulate.
  • Medical basics: Tourniquet, seal kit, stim; designate a casualty collection point.

Survival Protocol

  • Get out of open sightlines: Move to hard cover (durasteel, bulkheads, natural rock).
  • Break sensors & targeting: Use smoke, glare, ionized dust; force misreads in droid optics.
  • Threat triage: Reduce B1 volume first; fix B2s from angles; save droidekas for ion/EMP.
  • De-sync their scripts: Maneuver off-axis; feint; funnel into crossfires and choke points.
  • Short-burst movement: Shift positions every 5–10 seconds; never dash across open ground unprotected.
  • Shield counters: Ion or EMP to drop bubbles; only then apply concentrated blaster fire.
  • Watch the flanks: Expect aggressive swings; reinforce or withdraw before encirclement.
  • Coordinate allies: Sync with Jedi or clones; let them pin high-threat units while you maneuver.
  • Smart evacuation: Multi-route egress to rally points; avoid bottlenecks and predictable corridors.
  • After-contact checks: Account for civilians, mark hazards, log droid patterns for next action.

Environment Tactics

Urban

  • Vertical cover: Use bulkheads, pillars, and staggered corners to limit arcs.
  • Ambush lanes: Set traps in narrow passages; force droids into kill zones.
  • Light control: Backlight droidekas; use reflective debris to confuse sensors.
  • Door discipline: Slice/lock doors behind you to delay pursuit.

Wilderness

  • Terrain masking: Ridges & boulders break line-of-fire; stay low along contours.
  • Noise management: Short comms; avoid electronic signatures that draw patrols.
  • Decoys: Heat sources or moving fabric to misdirect aim routines.
  • Route layering: Prepare primary, alternate, contingency, and emergency paths.

Advanced Tactics

Anti-armor, suppression, deception & more
  • Anti-armor: Ion charges on access hatches; aim for joints & exhaust ports.
  • Suppression: Staggered fire to pin heads; maneuver a second element off-axis.
  • Deception: Sound loops & false beacons to pull patrols; strike the rear echelons.
  • Sensor flooding: Aerosolized particulates or mirrored shrapnel to force algorithms to stall.
  • Counter-encirclement: Pre-brief a “break glass” rally and punch through the thinnest segment.

Coordination Guidelines

  • Task pairing: Assign elements (fix, flank, support, med) and keep roles clean.
  • Tempo control: Push when droids stall; disengage when shields or mass fire spike.
  • Clone integration: Cross-train signals; agree on sectors & breach timing.
  • Civilians first: Move non-combatants early through secured corridors.
Plo Koon Spotlight

Jedi Master Plo Koon—calm, precise, and absolutely clutch under pressure—excels at defensive command, orderly withdrawals, and protecting civilians. If he’s present, align your movements to his positioning: let him fix high-threat units, then you exploit openings with disciplined flanks.

Evacuation & After-Action

Evac

  • Stagger routes: Avoid single corridors; pre-brief alternates.
  • Beacon discipline: Use low-power markers; don’t advertise to sensors.
  • Rear guard: Assign suppression while main body withdraws.

After-Action

  • Roll call: Account for all personnel and civilians.
  • Damage control: Treat, triage, stabilize; log hazards & munitions.
  • Intel share: Record droid patterns and shield timings for next contact.

Mistakes to Avoid

  • Crossing open ground: Without smoke or suppression—hard no.
  • Ignoring shields: Firing at active droideka bubbles wastes time and ammo.
  • Static defense: Droids love fixed targets; keep the tempo dynamic.
  • Comms overflow: Long transmissions invite triangulation and swarm pressure.
